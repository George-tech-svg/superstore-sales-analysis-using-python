**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**



The analysis of the Superstore’s 2011–2014 sales data reveals a business with a strong positive growth trajectory, but with significant opportunities to optimize marketing, inventory, and pricing strategies, especially regarding discounts.



The most critical finding is that November and December consistently outperform all other months, representing the peak holiday shopping season. However, the data also shows a clear negative correlation between discounts and profit, indicating that aggressive discounting is reducing overall profitability.



To maximize profit, the Superstore should focus on high-opportunity periods (Q4), high-value customer segments (Home Office), and high-margin product categories (Technology), while adopting a more disciplined approach to discounting.



**KEY PERFORMANCE SUMMARIES**



**Time-Based Performance (Best Times to Advertise**)



Monthly Trend: Sales are highly seasonal. September, November, and December consistently show the highest sales volumes across all four years. February is the slowest month.



Best Months for Advertising: November and December are the peak months. Marketing campaigns should begin in September to build momentum toward the holiday season.



Best Day of the Week: Friday records the highest number of orders, while Sunday is the slowest day.



**Product and Category Performance**



Most Profitable Category: Technology leads with a total profit of 663,778.73, followed by Office Supplies with 518,473.83.



Top-Selling Products: Smartphones dominate sales. The Apple Smart Phone (Full Size) is the top-selling product, with total sales of 86,935.78.



Loss-Making Products: Some high-tech items generate losses. The Cubify CubeX 3D Printer Double Head Print is the most unprofitable product, with a loss of -9,239.97.



**Customer Segment and Geography**



Most Profitable Segment: Home Office customers have the highest average profit margin at 5.29%, making them the most valuable segment per sale.



Top Country: The United States generates the highest sales compared to all other countries.



**Correlation and Pricing Analysis**



Discount vs Profit: There is a moderate negative correlation (-0.44) between discounts and profit. This means higher discounts are associated with lower profits, indicating that current discount strategies may be reducing profitability.



**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS**



A. Marketing and Promotions (When to Spend)



Front-Load Holiday Marketing: Launch major holiday campaigns in September instead of waiting until November. This helps capture early demand.



Friday Promotions: Introduce weekly Friday promotions, since it is the busiest day. Send promotional messages on Thursday evening or Friday morning.



Address Slow Periods: Introduce special campaigns such as a February sale or clearance event to boost performance during slow months.



**B. Inventory and Product Strategy (What to Sell)**



Prepare for Q4 Demand: Increase inventory levels, especially for Technology products and top-selling smartphones, before the holiday season.



Review Loss-Making Products: Analyze the least profitable products and decide whether to discontinue, renegotiate costs, or bundle them with profitable items.



Product Bundling: Combine popular products with slower-moving items to increase overall sales and reduce excess inventory.



**C. Pricing and Discount Strategy (How to Price)**



Limit Discounts: Introduce a policy to cap discounts at around 15–20% for most products.



Test Pricing Strategies: Run controlled tests with different discount levels (such as 5%, 10%, and 15%) to determine the most profitable approach.



Focus on Value: Instead of emphasizing discounts, highlight product value, especially for Home Office customers.



**D. Customer Targeting (Who to Focus On)**



Home Office Segment: Develop targeted campaigns and loyalty programs for Home Office customers, including benefits like faster delivery and early product access.



Corporate Customers: Improve engagement with corporate clients by offering bulk purchase benefits and streamlined billing options.
